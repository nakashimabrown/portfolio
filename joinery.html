Ming Joinery on the CNC
Solo project
Designed and built by
Hugo Nakashima-Brown
This spring, I am conducting a case study on adapting Ming joints for the “digital chisel” of the CNC router—developing a computational grammar that preserves historical joinery logic within accessible digital fabrication workflows.
These joints scale from furniture to architecture, enabling flat-pack assemblies and reusable systems. Three-axis CNC workflows make this logic widely accessible.

Two-sided registration is achievable even on a hobbyist CNC, allowing angled mortises by holding workpieces vertically against the spoilboard.
During a Spring fellowship, I aim to refine toolpaths, fixturing, and cosmetic roundovers on a larger 3-axis machine with greater Z-clearance.
